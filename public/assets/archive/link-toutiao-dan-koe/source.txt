I – You aren’t where you want to be because you aren’t the person who would be thereI – 你之所以未达理想之境，是因为你尚未成为能身处其中之人

When it comes to New Year’s resolutions, people only focus on one of the two requirements for success:谈及新年决心，人们往往只关注成功所需的两大要素之一：

1. Changing your actions to make progress toward the goal (least important, second order)改变行动以向目标迈进（最不重要，属次要层面）
2. Changing who you are so that your behavior naturally follows (most important, first order)改变自己，让行为自然随之改变（最为关键，优先处理）

Most people set a surface-level goal, hype themselves up to remain disciplined for the first few weeks, then go back to their old ways without much struggle, because they were trying to build a great life on a rotting foundation.大多数人设定的只是肤浅的目标，在前几周还能激励自己保持自律，然后就毫无挣扎地回到老样子，因为他们试图在一个腐烂的地基上建造美好的生活。

If this doesn’t make sense, let’s run through an example.如果这听起来不太明白，我们来看一个例子。

Think of somebody successful. It can be a bodybuilder with a great physique, a founder/CEO worth hundreds of millions, or a charismatic dude who can chat up a group without a shred of anxiety entering his mind space.想想某个成功人士。可以是一个拥有完美身材的健美运动员，身价数亿的创始人或 CEO，或是一个魅力十足、能在人群里侃侃而谈毫无焦虑的家伙。

To some people, my own lifestyle seems a bit extreme and disciplined. To me, it’s natural, and I don’t say that to contrast it with any other kind of lifestyle. I simply enjoy living this way. When my mom tells me that I should take a break, go out, and have some fun... I hold my tongue from telling her, “If I weren’t having fun, why would I be doing what I’m doing?”在某些人看来，我的生活方式或许有些极端且自律。但对我而言，这再自然不过，我这么说并非为了对比其他生活方式。我只是单纯享受这样活着。当我母亲建议我该休息放松、外出玩乐时…我忍着没告诉她："如果我不乐在其中，又何必坚持现在所做的一切呢？"

Do not take this next sentence lightly.接下来的这句话，请不要掉以轻心。

If you want a specific outcome in life, you must have the lifestyle that creates that outcome long before you reach it.如果你想在生活中达到某个特定的结果，你必须在实现它之前，就拥有能够带来这个结果的生活方式 。

If someone says they want to lose 30 pounds, I often don’t believe them. Not because I don’t think they are capable, but because there are too many times when that same person says “they can’t wait until they’re done losing weight so they can start to enjoy life again.” I hate to break it to you, but if you don’t adopt the lifestyle that led to you losing the weight, for life, and find a reason with a higher gravitational pull than the one tying you to your previous ways, then you will go straight back to where you started, and you can unhappily say that you wasted the resource you will never get back: time.如果有人声称他们想减掉 30 磅，我通常不会相信。这并不是因为我觉得他们做不到，而是因为这样的人常常会说“他们等不及减完肥，这样就能重新开始享受生活”。我不得不告诉你，如果你不采纳那种让你减重的生活方式，并将其终身保持，并且找到一个比你以往生活方式更具吸引力的理由，那么你会直接回到起点，并且只能不愉快地承认你浪费了永远无法追回的资源：时间。

When you truly change yourself, all of your habits that don’t move the needle toward your goal become disgusting, because you have a deep and profound awareness of what kind of life those actions compound into. You are okay with your current standards because you are not fully aware of what they are or what they lead to. We will discuss how to uncover this, but we need to build up to that.当你真正改变自己时，所有无法推动你实现目标的习惯都会变得令人厌恶，因为你深刻意识到那些行为最终将累积成怎样的生活。你之所以安于现状，是因为你并未完全认清这些标准是什么，又将导致何种结果。我们会探讨如何揭示这一切，但这需要循序渐进地展开。

You say you want to change. You say you want to “become financially free” and “get healthy,” but your actions show otherwise for a reason. And it goes a lot deeper than you think.你声称想要改变。你说渴望"实现财务自由"并"获得健康"，但你的行为却出于某种原因表现出相反的倾向。这背后的根源远比你以为的更为深邃。

# II – You aren’t where you want to be because you don’twantto be there第二篇章——你未能抵达理想的彼岸，只因你内心并未真正向往

Trust only movement. Life happens at the level of events, not of words. Trust movement.唯有行动值得信赖。生命的真相蕴藏在事件进程中，而非言语表象里。请坚信行动的力量。– Alfred Adler- 阿尔弗雷德·阿德勒

If you want to change who you are, you must understand how the mind works so that you can start to reprogram it.如果你想改变自己，就必须理解心智是如何运作的 ，这样才能开始重新编程它。

The first step to understanding the mind is to understand that all behavior is goal-oriented. When you think about it, this is kinda obvious, but when we dig into it, most people don’t want to hear it.理解心智的第一步，是要认识到所有行为都是目标导向的。仔细想想，这其实挺明显的，但当我们深入探究时，大多数人却不愿接受这一点。

You take a step forward because you want to reach a certain location.你向前迈出一步，是因为你想要到达某个地方。

You scratch your nose because you want to make the itch go away.你抓鼻子是因为想让那种瘙痒感消失。

Those ones are clear, but most of the time, your goals are unconscious. You may not realize that when you sit on the couch in the middle of the day, you are trying to burn time before your next responsibility, as one simple example.这些目标是很明显的，但大多数时候，你的目标是无意识的。你可能没有意识到，当你白天坐在沙发上时，你只是在试图消磨时间，直到下一个任务到来——这只是众多例子中的一个。

On an even more unconscious and complex level, you pursue goals that can harm you, but you justify your actions in a way that is socially acceptable and doesn’t make you seem like a loser.在更深层、更无意识的复杂层面上，你追求的目标可能对你有害，但你用社会接受的方式为自己的行为辩解，这样你就不会显得像个失败者。

As an example, if you can’t stop procrastinating your work, you may justify it with the fact that you “lack discipline,” but in reality, you are attempting to achieve a goal like you always are. In this case, that goal could be to protect yourself from the judgment that comes from finishing and sharing your work.举个例子，如果你无法停止拖延工作，你可能会以“缺乏自律”为由自我辩解，但实际上你像往常一样试图达成一个目标。在这种情况下，这个目标可能是保护自己免受完成并分享工作后可能招致的评判。

If you say you want to quit your dead-end job, but stay in it without any real reason, you may start to think you don’t have enough courage, or that you were never really a “risk taker,” but the truth is that you are pursuing the goal of safety, predictability, and an excuse to not look like a failure to everyone else in your life who also works a dead-end job.如果你想辞去那份毫无前途的工作，却又找不出真正留下的理由，你或许会开始怀疑自己缺乏勇气，或者认为自己从来不是个“敢于冒险的人”。但真相是，你其实在追求一种安全感、可预见性，以及一个借口——为了让那些同样从事着毫无前途工作的亲友眼中，你不至于显得是个失败者。

The lesson here is that real change requires changing your goals.这里的启示是，真正的转变需要转变你的目标。

I don’t mean setting some surface level goal because the act of doing that serves an unconscious goal that is actually harming you. That’s been ran through enough in the productivity space. I mean changing your point of view. Because that’s what a goal is. A goal is a projection into the future that acts as a lens of perception which allows you to notice information, ideas, and resources that aid in you achieving that goal.我指的不是设定某个表层目标， 因为这种做法其实是在迎合一个无意识中损害你的目标 。关于这一点，效率领域已经讨论得够多了。我指的是改变你的视角。 因为这才是目标的本质。目标是对未来的一个投射，它充当了一种感知的透镜，让你能够注意到那些有助于实现目标的信息、想法和资源。

Now let’s dig a bit deeper, because if you don’t understand this, it only becomes more difficult to get out.现在让我们深入探讨一下，因为如果不理解这一点，想要摆脱困境只会更加困难。

III – You aren’t where you want to be because you’re afraid to be thereIII – 你未能到达理想之境，只因你害怕身处其中

The important thing for you to remember is that it does not matter in the least how you got the idea or where it came from. You may never have met a professional hypnotist. You may never have been formally hypnotized. But if you have accepted an idea - from yourself, your teachers, your parents, friends, advertisements, from any other source - and further, if you are firmly convinced that idea is true, it has the same power over you as the hypnotist’s words have over the hypnotized subject.你必须记住的关键点在于，你是如何产生这个想法或它从何而来，这完全无关紧要。你可能从未见过专业的催眠师。你可能从未被正式催眠过。但如果你接受了一个想法——无论是来自你自己、你的老师、你的父母、朋友、广告，还是任何其他来源——更进一步，如果你坚信这个想法是真实的，那么它对你就如同催眠师的话语对催眠对象一样，具有同样的支配力。– Maxwell Maltz– 麦克斯韦·马尔茨

Here’s how you’ve become who you are today, and how you will become who you will be tomorrow. This is the anatomy of identity:这就是你如何成为今日之你，并如何塑造明日之你。这是身份的构成解剖：

1. You want to achieve a goal你渴望达成一个目标
2. You perceive reality through the lens of that goal你通过那个目标的视角来感知现实
3. You only notice “important” information and ideas that allows you to achieve that goal (learning)你只关注那些能助你实现目标的“重要”信息与想法（学习过程）
4. You act toward that goal and receive feedback that you are progressing toward it你朝着那个目标行动，并收到正在向目标靠近的反馈
5. You repeat that behavior until it becomes automatic and unconscious (conditioning)你重复这种行为，直到它变得自动且无意识（条件反射）
6. That behavior becomes a part of who you think you are (”I am the type of person who...”)该行为成为你自我认知的一部分（“我是那种……的人”）
7. You defend your identity to maintain psychological consistency你捍卫你的身份认同以保持心理一致性
8. Your identity shapes new goals, restarting the cycle, and if that identity is disadvantageous toward a good life, this gets bad very quick你的身份塑造新目标，重启循环；倘若这个身份不利于美好生活，局面就会迅速恶化

The unfortunate reality is that you must break the cycle between steps 6 and 7, but this process starts when you are a child.不幸的现实是，你必须打破第6和第7步之间的循环，但这个过程在你还是个孩子的时候就已经开始了。

You have the goal of survival.你以生存为目标。

You are dependent on your parents to teach you how to survive. You had to conform. And since the way most people teach is through reward and punishment, unless you adopt their beliefs and values, you will be punished. You don’t actually think for yourself until you see through this.你依赖父母教导你如何生存。你必须顺从。由于大多数人通过奖励和惩罚进行教导，除非采纳他们的信念与价值观，否则你将受到惩罚。除非看透这一点，你实际上并未为自己思考。

But your parents have also gone through this process throughout their entire lives. That’s where it can get dangerous. Your parents, unless they broke the pattern themselves, were conditioned by the culturally accepted ideas of success from the Industrial age. They also carry the best and worst conditioning from their parents and their parents’ parents.但你的父母也同样经历了这一生的过程。这其中的危险之处就在于此。你的父母，除非他们自己打破了模式，否则都被工业化时代中文化上被接受的成功理念所塑造。他们也继承了来自父母以及祖父母身上最好和最糟的印记。

To take it a layer deeper, once you fulfill your physical survival needs (which is quite easy to do in today’s world, you’re practically born into safety), you start to survive on the conceptual or ideological level. You may not try to protect and reproduce your body, but you absolutely protect and reproduce your mind. It’s not difficult to see the war of ideas on the internet, and the participants are individual and group identities.进一步而言，当你满足基本生存需求（这在现代社会相当容易实现，人们几乎生来就处于安全环境）之后，你便开始在概念或意识形态层面求存。你可能不再竭力维护肉体延续，但必定会全力捍卫并传播自己的思想观念。网络上的观点之争随处可见，参与者正是由个体与集体认同构成的身份群体。

When your body feels threatened, you go into fight or flight.当你的身体感受到威胁时，你会进入战斗或逃跑状态。

When your identity feels threatened, the same thing happens.当你的身份认同受到威胁时，同样的反应也会发生。

If you are heavily identified with a political ideology (by the process we talked about just before), you will feel threatened when someone challenges your beliefs. You literally feel the stress. You feel, emotionally, like you were just slapped in the face. Since most people don’t analyze their emotions for truth, you tend to get stuck in echo chambers and double down on claims that harm yourself and others.如果你强烈认同某种政治意识形态（通过我们先前讨论的认同过程），当有人质疑你的信念时，你会感受到切实的威胁。这种压力真实可感，情绪上就像被当面掌掴。由于大多数人不会理性分析情绪背后的真相，往往陷入信息茧房，固执于那些既伤害他人又损害自己的主张。

If you were raised in a religious household, and did not think for yourself, you will fight and attack others who threaten your psychological safety within that little bubble.若你成长于宗教家庭且缺乏独立思考，你将会攻击那些威胁到你心理安全区的人。

The same thing happens when you unconsciously see yourself as a lawyer, a gamer, or somebody else who would not take the actions to achieve a better life.当你无意识地将自己定义为律师、游戏玩家或其他不愿采取行动改善生活的身份时，同样的情况也会发生。

# IV – The life you want lies within a specific level of mind四、理想生活源于特定思维层次

The mind evolves through predictable stages over time.心智会随时间推移经历可预见的成长阶段。

When you’re born, you’re like a little survival sponge that absorbs whatever beliefs you can (which are heavily dictated by your culture) so that you can feel safe and secure. And if you don’t be careful, your mind may crystalize and it may make it difficult to live a meaningful life.人出生时如同生存海绵，会吸收任何能获取的信念（这些信念很大程度上受文化支配）以获得安全感。若不加以注意，心智可能固化，进而难以活出有意义的人生。

This has been documented enough in models like Maslow’s Hierarchy, Greuter’s stages of ego development, and Spiral Dynamics, each building off of one another, but it’s also not difficult to observe in society.这一点在马斯洛需求层次、格雷特的自我发展模型以及螺旋动力学等理论中已有充分记载，它们相互借鉴，但在社会中观察这种现象也并不困难。

I’ve talked about these many times, and synthesized them into my own Human 3.0 model, but here’s the 80/20 of the 9 stages of ego development as a refresher (because repetition helps reveal things you didn’t notice before, and there are new people reading these letters):我已多次谈及这些内容，并将其整合进自己的人类 3.0 模型 ，但作为复习，以下是自我发展九阶段中关键的 80/20 原则（因为重复能帮助发现先前忽略的细节，而且这些信件也有新读者）：

自我发展的 9 个阶段

1. Impulsive — No separation between impulse and action. Black and white thinking. I.e. A toddler hits when angry because the feeling and the behavior are the same thing.冲动型 —— 冲动与行动之间毫无界限。非黑即白的思维。 例如，一个蹒跚学步的孩子在生气时会打人，因为对他们来说，感受和行为是同一回事。
2. Self-Protective — The world is dangerous and you learn to look out for yourself. I.e. A kid learns to hide report cards, lie about chores, and figure out what adults want to hear.自我保护型 ——世界充满危险，你学会为自己设防。 例如：孩子学会藏成绩单、谎报家务完成情况、揣摩大人想听什么。
3. Conformist — You are your group and its rules feel like reality itself. I.e. Someone who genuinely cannot fathom why anyone would vote differently than their family or group.从众型 ——你与所属群体融为一体，群体规则对你而言即是现实。 例如：有人完全无法理解为何会有人投票选择与家族或团体不同的立场。
4. Self-Aware — You notice you have an inner life that doesn’t match the exterior. I.e. Sitting in church and realizing you’re not sure you believe what everyone around you seems to believe, but not knowing what to do with that feeling yet.自我觉醒型 ——你觉察到内在自我与外在表现存在割裂。 例如：坐在教堂里突然意识到，自己并不确定是否相信周围人笃信的事物，却不知该如何处理这种感受。
5. Conscientious — You build your own system of principles and hold yourself accountable to them. I.e. Leaving your family’s religion after careful study and adopting a personal philosophy you can defend, or building a career plan with clear milestones because you believe the right effort yields the right results.恪守原则型 ——你建立起自己的原则体系并为其负责。 例如：经过深入研究后放弃家族信仰，转而信奉能自洽的个人哲学；或是制定包含明确里程碑的职业规划，因为你相信正确努力终会带来应有成果。
6. Individualist — You see that your principles were shaped by context and start holding them more loosely. I.e. Realizing your political views have more to do with where you grew up than objective truth, or noticing that your ambitious career goals were really about earning your father’s approval.个人主义者 —— 你意识到自身的原则皆受环境塑造，于是开始更为灵活地秉持它们。 例如，你认识到自己的政治观点更多与成长背景相关，而非客观真理；或是注意到那些雄心勃勃的职业目标，实则是为了赢得父亲的认可。
7. Strategist — You work with systems while aware of your own involvement in them. I.e. Leading an organization while actively questioning your own blind spots, or engaging in politics knowing your perspective is partial and shaped by bias you can’t fully see.策略家 ——你在与体系共事的同时，清楚意识到自己身在其中。 例如，领导一个组织时积极质疑自身的盲区，或参与政治时明白你的视角是片面的，并受制于自身无法完全看清的偏见。
8. Construct-Aware — You see all frameworks, including your identity, as useful fictions. I.e. Holding your spiritual beliefs with metaphorically not literally, knowing the map is not the territory, or watching yourself play the role of “founder” or “thought leader” with a kind of gentle amusement.构建意识 ——你将所有框架（包括自我认同）都视为有用的虚构。 例如：以隐喻而非字面意义持守精神信仰，知晓地图并非疆域本身，或是带着温和的趣味审视自己扮演“创始人”或“思想领袖”的角色。
9. Unitive — Separation between self and life dissolves. I.e. Work, rest, and play feel like the same thing. There’s no one left who needs to become something, just presence responding to what arises.统一性 ——自我与生活的区隔逐渐消融。 例如：工作、休憩与娱乐产生同质体验。不再有人需要刻意成为某种存在，只有临在意识应对着当下发生的一切。

For most people reading this, I would assume you hover between 4 and 8, which is a huge gap. Those closer to 8 are reading this are doing so to either learn something or pass time. Those closer to 4 are really looking for a change. You feel like you are meant for more, but you can’t make sense of everything yet, because there’s obviously a lot at play.对于大多数读到这里的读者，我推测你的状态大致在 4 到 8 之间浮动，这个区间相当宽泛。接近 8 的读者可能只是为了学习新知或消磨时间而阅读。而接近 4 的读者，则是真正渴望改变。你感觉自己本应成就更多，却仍理不清头绪，因为显然有太多因素在起作用。

The good thing is, it doesn’t really matter what stage you are in, because moving through any of them follows a pattern.值得庆幸的是，你目前处于哪个阶段其实并不重要，因为每个阶段的进阶都遵循着相似的规律。

# V – Intelligence is the ability to get what you want out of life第五项——智慧是获得人生所求的能力

The only real test of intelligence is if you get what you want out of life.智力唯一真正的检验标准是：你是否从生活中得到了你想要的。– Naval Ravikant– 纳瓦尔·拉维肯特

There is a formula for success.成功是有公式的。

One ingredient is agency.一个要素是自主力 。

One ingredient is opportunity (which many people like to mistake as “privilege” - because they the other ingredients).一个要素是机遇 （许多人常将其误认为“特权”——因为他们忽略了其他要素）。

The last ingredient is intelligence.最后一个要素是智慧 。

If you have high agency but low opportunity, it doesn’t matter how likely you are to act toward a goal, because it isn’t a goal that will bear much fruit.如果你拥有高能动性但机会匮乏，那么无论你朝着目标行动的可能性有多大，都无关紧要，因为这并非一个能结出丰硕成果的目标。

If you have opportunity and agency but low intelligence, then you will never be fully able to benefit from that opportunity.如果你拥有机会和能动性，但智慧不足，那么你将永远无法充分利用这些机会。

First, we’ve talked about agency before here. In terms of opportunity, I can’t tell you to change your physical location, but if you don’t see the abundance of digital opportunity right in front of you, I don’t know what to tell you.首先，我们之前在这里讨论过能动性。就机会而言，我不能让你改变你的物理位置，但如果你看不到眼前丰富的数字机会，我不知道该对你说什么。

With that said, I want to focus on what intelligence is in the context of these two other ingredients and this letter.在此前提下，我想聚焦探讨智慧的本质 ——结合另外两个要素以及本文的语境。

Cybernetics comes from the greek word kybernetikos which means “to steer” or “good at steering.”控制论一词源于希腊语 kybernetikos，意为“掌舵”或“善于掌舵”。

It’s also known as “the art of getting what you want.”它也被称为"获得所求的艺术"。

So, if Naval’s definition of intelligence is getting what you want out of life, understanding cybernetics helps you do that much faster.因此，若按 Naval 的定义，智能即为获得人生所求，那么理解控制论将助你更快实现这一目标。

Cybernetics illustrates the properties of intelligent systems.控制论揭示了智能系统的特性。

- To have a goal.关键在于设定目标。
- Act toward that goal.朝着那个目标行动。
- Sense where you are.感知自己身处何处。
- Compare it to the goal.将其与目标进行比较。
- And act again based on that feedback.然后基于这一反馈再次行动。

智能系统的进化路径

You can judge intelligence based on the system’s ability to iterate and persist with trial and error.评判一个系统的智能程度，可依据其在试错中迭代与坚持的能力。

A ship blown off course that corrects toward its destination. A thermostat sensing a change in heat and turning on. The pancreas excreting insulin after blood glucose spikes.一艘偏航的船舶重新校准航向驶向目的地。恒温器感应温度变化而启动。血糖升高后胰腺分泌胰岛素。

What does this have to do with getting what you want out of life?这与实现人生所求有何关联？

Everything.世间万物。

Acting, sensing, comparing, and understanding the system from a meta-perspective is fundamental to high intelligence.从元认知层面进行行动、感知、比较并理解系统运作规律，是构成高智商的根基。

High intelligence is the ability to iterate, persist, and understand the big picture. The mark of low intelligence is the inability to learn from your mistakes.高智商体现在能够迭代、坚持并理解全局。 低智商的标志则是无法从错误中吸取教训。

Low-intelligence people get stuck on problems rather than solving them. They hit a roadblock and quit. Like a writer who fails to build a readership and quits because they lack the ability to try new things, experiment, and figure out a process that works for them (to think that there isn’t an effective process you can create is verifiably false, no matter your limiting beliefs, hence being low intelligence.)低认知者常固着于问题而非解决之道。遭遇阻碍便轻言放弃。如同那些未能积累读者就弃笔的作者，他们缺乏尝试新事物、探索实验、构建专属工作流程的能力（认为无法创建有效流程的想法经得起验证是错误的，任何自我设限的信念都印证了认知的局限性）。

High intelligence is realizing any problem can be solved on a large enough timescale. The reality is that you can achieve any goal you set your mind to. This isn’t something that can be disproven within reason.高智商的核心在于认识到任何问题只要时间足够长都能被解决。事实就是，只要你下定决心，任何目标都能实现——这在理性范围内是无法被证伪的。

Intelligence is realizing that there is a series of choices you can make which lead to achieving the goal you want. You understand that ideas are hierarchical and that you can’t go from papyrus to Google docs in one fell swoop. Even if that goal is impossible right now, you simply don’t have the resources – which may be invented over the next few years – to achieve that thing.智慧在于认识到存在一系列你可以做出的选择，这些选择将引导你实现想要的目标。你明白想法是有层次结构的，无法一蹴而就从纸莎草纸直接跳到谷歌文档。即使那个目标目前看似不可能实现，你只是缺乏资源——这些资源可能在未来的几年里被创造出来——来完成那件事。

When I talk about “goals,” and as I will continue repeating, I am not speaking from the typical lens of self-help, although that’s a helpful lens to adopt at times.当谈到"目标"时，必须重申——我并非从传统自助视角出发，尽管那种视角偶尔也有其价值。

I am speaking from the lens of teleology or the Greek kosmos – that everything serves a purpose. That everything is a part of a greater whole.我是从目的论 ，或者希腊语 kosmos（宇宙）的视角出发——即万物皆有目的 ，一切都是更伟大整体的一部分。

Goals determine how you see the world.目标决定着你如何看待这个世界。

Goals determine what you consider “success” or “failure.”目标决定了你对"成功"或"失败"的定义。

You can try to “enjoy the journey,” but if you pursue the wrong goal, you will not enjoy it.你可以试着"享受过程"，但如果追求错误的目标，你将无法享受它。

Your mind is the operating system for reality.你的心智是现实的操作系统。

That system is composed of goals.这个系统由目标构成。

For most people, those goals are assigned to them. Programmed like lines of code in your psyche.对大多数人来说，这些目标是他人指定的。就像是编码一样被编程进了你的心理。

Go to school. Get the job. Get offended. Play victim. Retire at 65.上学。找个工作。被人冒犯。扮演受害者。65 岁退休。

A known path that doesn’t work.一条已知的、却行不通的路。

To become more intelligent, you must:想要变得更聪明，你必须：

- Dive into the unknown潜入未知
- Set new, higher goals to expand your mind设定更高目标，拓展思维边界
- Embrace the chaos and allow for growth拥抱混乱，允许成长
- Study the generalized principles of nature研究大自然的普遍法则
- Become a deep generalist成为一名深刻的多面手

# VI – How to launch into a completely new life (in 1 day)六 – 如何在一天内开启崭新人生

The best periods of my life always came after a period of getting absolutely fed up with the lack of progress I was making.我生命中最精彩的阶段，总在彻底厌倦进步停滞之后到来。

How do you dig into your mind?如何深入挖掘你的内心？

How do you become aware of your conditioning?你如何意识到自己的制约？

How do you reach profound insights and truths that change the trajectory of your life?如何才能触及足以改变人生轨迹的深刻洞见与真理？

Through the simple, but often painful act of questioning.经由一个简单却又时常痛苦的行为： 质疑。

Something that so few people do, and you can tell by how they speak or give their thoughts on a specific topic. Questioning is thinking, and very few people do it.能做到这一点的人寥寥无几，只需听他们对特定话题的发言或想法就能分辨。提问即思考，但实践者甚少。

I want to give you a comprehensive protocol that you can use every year to reset your life and launch into a season of intense progress. This protocol helps you ask the right questions.我想给你一套完整的方案，你可以每年用它来重置生活，开启一段全力奋进的时节。这套方案能帮你提出正确的问题。

These questions will cover the macro to the micro: where you want to be, what you need to do to get there, and what you can do immediately to start moving the needle toward that reality.这些问题将涵盖宏观到微观：你想达到的位置，实现目标需要采取的行动，以及可以立即着手推进现实转变的具体步骤。

This will require one full day to complete, so I recommend you follow along with the exact protocol. You will need a pen, paper, and an open mind.这需要一整天的时间来完成，因此我建议你严格按照这一流程执行。你需要准备好一支笔、一张纸，以及开放的心态。

When I observe patterns in people who successfully flip their identity, it happens fast after a build up of tension. Specifically, I’ve noticed 3 phases that people then to go through.通过观察那些成功转变身份者的行为模式，我发现这种转变往往在累积了足够的内在压力后迅速发生。具体来说，我注意到人们通常会经历以下三个阶段。

1. Dissonance – They feel like they don’t belong in their current life, and become sufficiently fed up with their lack of progress.失调感 ——他们感到自己与当下的生活格格不入，并对停滞不前感到彻底厌倦。
2. Uncertainty – They don’t know what comes next, so they either experiment or get lost and feel worse.迷茫期 ——他们不知前路何方，于是要么尝试探索，要么迷失方向并陷入更糟的情绪。
3. Discovery – They discover what they want to pursue and make 6 years of progress in 6 months.觉醒时刻 ——他们找到了渴望追求的目标，并在六个月内取得了原本需要六年才能实现的进展。

So, our goal with this protocol is to help you reach the point of dissonance, navigate through uncertainty, and discover what it truly is that you want to achieve, so much so that the clarity is overwhelming and distractions no longer hold their weight.因此，我们通过这套方案的目标是帮助你抵达认知失调的边缘，引领你穿越不确定的迷雾，最终让你发现内心真正渴望实现的目标。直至这种清晰度足以压倒一切，而外界纷扰再也无法动摇你的决心。

This protocol is structured so that it can be completed in one day. In the morning, you do a psychological excavation to uncover your own hidden motives. During the day, you prompt yourself with interrupts to keep you out of autopilot and contemplate your life. At night, you synthesize the insights into a direction you will start to move in tomorrow.这套方案被设计成可以在一天内完成。早晨，进行一次心理挖掘以揭示你潜在的动机。白天，利用提示中断来让自己摆脱自动驾驶模式，深入思考生活。夜晚，将所得洞见整合成你从明天开始将要迈出的方向。

I cannot guarantee that this will work for everyone, because I cannot guarantee that everyone reading this is in the right chapter of their own story that would make these points impactful. You can’t place the climax at the start of the book and expect it to be interesting.我不能保证这对每个人都有效，因为我无法保证每个阅读本文的人正处于自己故事的正确章节，从而让这些观点产生深刻影响。你不能把高潮放在书的开头并指望它引人入胜。

Part 1) Morning – Psychological Excavation – Vision & Anti-Vision第一部分）清晨——心理发掘——愿景与反愿景

First we must create a new frame, or lens of perception, for your mind to operate from.首先，我们必须创建一个新的框架或感知视角，让你的心智得以运作。

This is like creating a new shell, leaving your old one, and slowly growing into it over time. It won’t feel like it fits at first. That’s a good thing.这就像是创造一个新的外壳，蜕去旧的外壳，然后逐渐适应它。一开始可能会感觉不太合身，但这其实是件好事。

Set aside 15-30 minutes (the length of one YouTube video... you can do it) to think about and answer these questions. Do not attempt to outsource this contemplation to AI. I want you to break past the limiter that is on your mind. If you can’t answer these immediately, come back to them later.预留 15-30 分钟（差不多相当于一个 YouTube 视频的长度……你完全可以做到），仔细思考并回答这些问题。切勿尝试将这项思考任务外包给 AI 处理。我希望你能突破自己思维上的限制。如果一时无法立即作答，稍后再回来完成。

1. What is the dull and persistent dissatisfaction you’ve learned to live with? Not the deep suffering but what you’ve learned to tolerate. (If you don’t hate it, you will tolerate it)什么是你已经学会与之共处的沉闷且持续的不满足感？不是那种深刻的痛苦，而是你已经学会忍受的东西。（如果你不讨厌它，你就会容忍它。）
2. What do you complain about repeatedly but never actually change? Write down the three complaints you’ve voiced most often in the past year.你反复抱怨却从未真正改变的是什么？写下过去一年中最常抱怨的三件事。
3. For each complaint: What would someone who watched your behavior (not your words) conclude that you actually want?每个抱怨：观察你行为（而非言语）的人，会得出你实际上想要什么结论？
4. What truth about your current life would be unbearable to admit to someone you deeply respect?你目前生活的哪些真相，是对你深为敬重之人难以启齿的？

Those questions are meant to make you aware of the pain in your current life. Now, we need to turn those into what I call an “anti-vision,” which is a brutal awareness of the life you do not want to live. That way, you can use that negative energy to aim your efforts in a positive direction and act from a place of intrinsic motivation.这些问题旨在让你意识到当前生活中的痛苦。现在，我们需要将其转化为我所说的"反愿景"——即对自己不愿过的生活形成一种残酷的清醒认知。这样一来，你就能利用这种负面能量，将努力指向积极的方向，并在内在动力的驱动下采取行动。

1. If absolutely nothing changes for the next five years, describe an average Tuesday. Where do you wake up? What does your body feel like? What’s the first thing you think about? Who’s around you? What do you do between 9am and 6pm? How do you feel at 10pm?假设未来五年一成不变，描述一个寻常的星期二。你从哪里醒来？身体感觉如何？首先想到什么？身边有谁？上午 9 点到下午 6 点之间做什么？晚上 10 点时心情怎样？
2. Now do it but for ten years. What have you missed? What opportunities closed? Who gave up on you? What do people say about you when you’re not in the room?那么，坚持十年后再看。你会错过什么？哪些机遇之门已经关闭？谁会对你失去信心？当你不在场时，人们会如何评价你？
3. You’re at the end of your life. You lived the safe version. You never broke the pattern. What was the cost? What did you never let yourself feel, try, or become?假设你已走到生命尽头。这一生你始终谨小慎微，从未打破既定的生活模式。代价是什么？有哪些感受你从未体验过？哪些尝试你从未付诸行动？哪些可能的自我你从未实现？
4. Who in your life is already living the future you just described? Someone five, ten, twenty years ahead on the same trajectory? What do you feel when you think about becoming them?你认识的人里，是否已经有人活出了你刚刚描述的未来？有没有人在同样的道路上领先你五年、十年甚至二十年？想到要成为他们那样的人时，你内心是什么感受？
5. What identity would you have to give up to actually change? (”I am the type of person who...”) What would it cost you socially to no longer be that person?要实现真正的改变，你需要舍弃什么样的旧有身份？（比如："我是那种......的人"）不再扮演那个角色后，你在社交层面需要付出怎样的代价？
6. What is the most embarrassing reason you haven’t changed? The one that makes you sound weak, scared, or lazy rather than reasonable?阻碍你改变的最尴尬原因是什么？是那些让你听起来软弱、胆怯或懒惰而非理智的理由？
7. If your current behavior is a form of self-protection, what exactly are you protecting? And what is that protection costing you?如果你当下的行为是一种自我保护，那你究竟在保护什么？这种保护正在让你付出怎样的代价？

If you answered those truthfully, and if you are in the right chapter of your life, you will feel a deep sense of dis-ease and possibly disgust for how you are currently living. Now, we need to orient that energy in a positive direction. We need to create a minimum viable vision, because your vision is like a product. It starts out unclear, but with time and experience, it grows stronger and more potent.如果你如实回答了这些问题，并且正处在人生的关键阶段，你可能会对当前的生活方式感到深深的不安甚至厌恶。现在，我们需要将这股能量导向积极的方向。我们必须建立一个最低限度的可行愿景，因为你的愿景就像一件产品——最初或许模糊不清，但随着时间的推移和经验的积累，它会变得愈发清晰有力。

1. Forget practicality for a minute. If you could snap your fingers and be living a different life in three years, not what’s realistic, what you actually want? What does an average Tuesday look like? Same level of detail as question 5.暂时忘掉实用性。如果你打个响指就能在三年后过上截然不同的生活，不是考虑现实条件，而是你真正渴望的？一个普通的星期二会是什么样？细节程度请与问题 5 保持一致。
2. What would you have to believe about yourself for that life to feel natural rather than forced? Write the identity statement: “I am the type of person who...”怎样的自我认知才能让那种生活显得自然而非刻意？写下身份声明："我是那种……的人"
3. What is one thing you would do this week if you were already that person?如果你已经是那样的人，这周你会做一件什么事？

Answer all of those first thing in the morning tomorrow.明早起床第一件事就是回答所有这些问题。

Part 2) Throughout The Day – Interrupting Autopilot – Breaking Unconscious Patterns第二部分）全天执行——打断自动驾驶状态——打破无意识模式

These journaling exercises are cute, but we want real change.这些写日记练习虽可爱，但我们想要的是真正的改变。

Frankly, that’s not going to happen if you don’t break the current unconscious patterns that are keeping you the same.坦率地说，如果你不打破当前那些让你停滞不前的无意识模式，改变就不会发生。

Throughout the day, I want you to contemplate on everything you journaled in part one. Beyond that, I don’t want you to forget to contemplate. Please take this seriously. You aren’t going to change by doing the same thing for the rest of your life. You need to consciously force a pattern break.在这一整天里，我希望你仔细思考第一部分中所记录的一切。此外，请不要忘记去深思。请认真对待此事。如果余生都重复相同的行为，你将无法改变。你需要有意识地强行打破模式。

Take the time right now to create reminders or calendar events in your phone. Include the question in the reminder or event so that you can immediately start thinking about it.现在就花时间在你的手机上设置提醒或日历事件。在提醒或事件中包含问题，以便你能立即开始思考它。

The more random and non-conflicting with your schedule there are, the better.越是随机且不与你日程冲突，效果越好。

- 11:00am: What am I avoiding right now by doing what I’m doing?上午 11:00： 此刻我正在做的事，是在逃避什么？
- 1:30pm: If someone filmed the last two hours, what would they conclude I want from my life?下午 1:30： 如果有人拍下我过去两小时的生活，他们会认为我的人生追求是什么？
- 3:15pm: Am I moving toward the life I hate or the life I want?下午 3:15： 我正在靠近自己厌恶的生活，还是向往的生活？
- 5:00pm: What’s the most important thing I’m pretending isn’t important?下午 5:00： 我假装不重要的最重要之事究竟是什么？
- 7:30pm: What did I do today out of identity protection rather than genuine desire? (Hint: it’s most things you do)晚上 7:30： 今天我做了哪些事是出于身份保护而非真正渴望？（提示：这包括你大部分日常行为）
- 9:00pm: When did I feel most alive today? When did I feel most dead?晚上九点： 今天我何时感到充满活力？何时感到死气沉沉？

To add a bit more fuel to the fire, schedule these questions during times where you are either commuting, walking, or lying around.若想让反思更深刻，不妨在通勤、散步或躺平时安排这些自省时刻。

- What would change if I stopped needing people to see me as [the identity you wrote in question 10]?如果我不再需要别人把我看作［你在问题 10 中写的身份］，会发生什么改变？
- Where in my life am I trading aliveness for safety?在我的生活中，哪些地方我为了安全而牺牲了活力？
- What’s the smallest version of the person I want to become that I could be tomorrow?我明天就可以成为的那个我想成为的人，他的最小版本是怎样的？

Part 3) Evening – Synthesizing Insight – Entering A Season Of Progress第三部分）傍晚——整合洞见——进入一个进步的阶段

If you followed that process, I would be surprised if you didn’t have at least one profound insight that could alter the course of your life. Now, we need to make those known, integrate them into who we are, and act on them to begin solidifying our journey to a new level of mind.如果你按照那个流程进行，哪怕只收获一个足以改变你人生轨迹的深刻洞见，我都不会感到意外。现在，我们需要将这些洞见明确化，将其融入我们的本质，并依据它们采取行动，从而开始在通往更高心智境界的旅途中稳固前行。

1. After today, what feels most true about why you’ve been stuck?经过今天之后，关于你为何陷入困境，你觉得最真实的原因是什么？
2. What is the actual enemy? Name it clearly. Not circumstances. Not other people. The internal pattern or belief that has been running the show.真正的敌人是什么？请清晰地命名它。不是环境，也不是他人。而是那些一直在背后操控你的内在模式或信念。
3. Write a single sentence that captures what you refuse to let your life become. This is your anti-vision compressed. It should make you feel something when you read it.用一句话概括你绝不允许自己的生活变成的模样。这就是你浓缩的反向愿景。当你读到它时，它应该能触动你的心弦。
4. Write a single sentence that captures what you’re building toward, knowing it will evolve. This is your vision MVP.用一个句子概括你正在努力实现的愿景，哪怕它还会变化。这就是你的愿景核心版。

Lastly, we need to create goals.最后，我们需要设立目标。

Again, these aren’t goals that you set for the sake of achievement, because goals are just projections. They are unreliable and make you feel bound to something that will inevitably change. Instead, think of goals as a point of view. A lens that you can exchange to enter the right state of mind to perform the action that will lead away from the life you don’t want. Do not worry about some kind of finish line, because as we will find, it doesn’t exist. Enjoyment is found in progress.再说一次，这些不是你为追求成就而设立的目标，因为目标只是预测。它们并不可靠，会让你感到被必然变化的事物所束缚。相反，将目标视为一种视角、一个滤镜，你可以换上它，进入正确的状态，采取行动，远离你不想要的生活。不必担心某种终点线，因为我们会发现它并不存在。乐趣就在进步的过程中。

1. One-year lens: What would have to be true in one year for you to know you’ve broken the old pattern? One concrete thing.一年视角： 要让你知道自己已经打破了旧模式，未来一年中必须发生哪一件具体的事？
2. One-month lens: What would have to be true in one month for the one-year lens to remain possible?月尺度视角： 要在一个月内实现什么，才能使年尺度目标保持可行性？
3. Daily lens: What are 2-3 actions you can timeblock tomorrow that the person you’re becoming would simply do?每日聚焦： 你能为明天的哪些 2-3 个行动安排专属时段，让你逐步成长为理想中的自己？

That was a lot.内容确实不少。

Hopefully it was helpful.希望这些内容对你有所助益。

But we have one last piece to lock it all in.不过，我们还有最后一步，将所有碎片完美拼合。

Stick with me.请继续跟随我的步伐。

# VII – Turn Your Life Into A Video Game第七步——将人生蜕变为一场游戏

The optimal state of inner experience is one in which there is order in consciousness. This happens when psychic energy—or attention—is invested in realistic goals, and when skills match the opportunities for action. The pursuit of a goal brings order in awareness because a person must concentrate attention on the task at hand and momentarily forget everything else.最优的心理体验状态是在意识中建立秩序。这种状态发生在精神能量（或称注意力）被投入到现实的目标中，且个人技能与行动机会相匹配时。追求目标能让意识保持有序，因为人们必须将注意力集中于眼前的任务，暂时忘却其他一切。– Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi– 米哈里·契克森米哈赖

You now have all of the components that lead to a good life.你现在已掌握了通往幸福生活的所有要素。

Now, it may be helpful to organize all of your insights into one coherent plan. Pull out a new page and write down these 6 components:现在，将所有这些洞见整合为一个连贯的计划会很有帮助。拿出一张新纸，写下这六个要素：

- Anti-vision – What is the bane of my existence, or the life I never want to experience again?反面愿景 ——我生命中的祸根是什么，或是我绝不愿再经历的生活？
- Vision – What is the ideal life that I think I want and can improve as I work toward it?理想愿景 ——我心中渴望并能在追求过程中不断完善的理想生活是怎样的？
- 1 year goal – What will my life look like in 1 year time, and is that closer to the life I want?一年目标 ——一年后我的生活会是怎样的？那是否更接近我想要的生活？
- 1 month project – What do I need to learn? What skills do I need to acquire? What can I build that will move me closer to the one year goal?月度计划 ——我需要学习什么？需要掌握哪些技能？我能构建什么来推动自己更接近年度目标？
- Daily levers – What are the priority, needle-moving tasks that bring my project closer to completion?每日杠杆 ——哪些是推动计划完成的关键优先任务？
- Constraints – What am I not willing to sacrifice to achieve my vision from the ground up?约束条件 ——为了彻底实现我的愿景，我不愿意牺牲什么？

Why is this so powerful?为什么这如此强大？

Because these components literally create your own little world. If you are meant to pursue this hierarchy of goals at this stage of your life, you will have no other option but to become obsessed. You will feel the pull to something greater. You will not see anything else as an option.因为这些组成部分实际上创造了你自己的小世界。如果你命中注定要在人生的这个阶段追求这个目标层级，你将别无选择，只能全情投入。你会感受到被某种更宏大的事物牵引。你将视其他一切为不存在的选项。

You turn your life into a video game.你将生活变成了一款电子游戏。

Because games are the poster child for obsession, enjoyment, and flow states. They have all the components that lead to focus and clarity, so if we reverse engineer what those components are, we can live in a state of deeper enjoyment, less distractions, and more success.因为游戏是痴迷、享受与心流的完美典范。它们具备所有引导专注与清晰度的要素，因此如果我们逆向解析这些要素是什么，我们就能生活在一个更享受、更少分心、更成功的状态之中。

Your vision is how you win. At least until the game evolves.你的愿景是制胜之道。 获胜的关键。至少在这场游戏演化之前如此。

Your anti-vision is what’s at stake. What happens if you lose or give up.你的反愿景是潜在风险 。如果你失败或放弃，将会发生什么。

Your 1 year goal is the mission. This is your sole priority in life.你的一年目标是你的人生使命 。这是你生活中唯一的重心。

Your 1 month project is the boss fight. How you gain XP and acquire loot.你的一月项目是一场 BOSS 战 。这是你获取经验和战利品的方式。

Your daily levers are the quests. The daily process that unlocks new opportunities.你的日常杠杆便是那些挑战任务 。这个日常过程能为你开启新的机遇之门。

Your constraints are the rules. The limitations that encourage creativity.你的限制就是 规则 。这些限制鼓励着创造力。

All of these act as a concentric set of circles, like a forcefield, that guard your mind from distractions and shiny objects.所有这些如同同心圆，像一道力场，保护你的心智不受干扰和诱人事物的影响。

The more you play the game, the stronger this force becomes, and soon enough it becomes who you are, and you wouldn’t have it any other way.你越投入这场游戏，这股力量就越强大，很快它就成了你的一部分，而你也不愿有任何改变。

全文完，如果你能读到这里，相信你一定像我一样有很多收获，剩下的就是将这个认知落实到行动中，就想文章中关于控制论（Cybernetics）的描述一样：去行动，去感知，去比较和调整，然后再次行动，过程中逐步调整，直到逐渐获得自己愿景中的生活。
